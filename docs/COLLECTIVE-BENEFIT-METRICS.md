# Collective Benefit Metrics

## Overview

Collective Benefit Metrics track how well Azora OS embodies Ubuntu philosophy ("I am because we are"). These metrics measure community impact, shared prosperity, collaborative action, and constitutional protection.

## Core Metrics

### 1. Community Engagement Score (0-100)

**What it measures**: Overall level of community participation and engagement

**Components**:
- Active users participating in community activities
- Frequency of community interactions
- Quality of community contributions
- Diversity of community members

**Target**: 75+  
**Frequency**: Daily  
**Dashboard**: Collective Benefit Dashboard

**How to improve**:
- Create more community events and activities
- Make participation easier and more rewarding
- Recognize and celebrate community contributions
- Gather feedback and respond to community needs

---

### 2. Wealth Distribution Index (Gini Coefficient)

**What it measures**: Equality of wealth distribution in the community

**Range**: 0 (perfect equality) to 1 (perfect inequality)

**Components**:
- Income distribution across users
- Wealth concentration
- Access to opportunities
- Economic mobility

**Target**: < 0.35 (more equal than most countries)  
**Frequency**: Weekly  
**Dashboard**: Collective Benefit Dashboard

**How to improve**:
- Implement Universal Basic Income (UBI)
- Share revenue with creators fairly
- Provide equal access to opportunities
- Support economic mobility

---

### 13. Overall Collective Benefit Score (0-100)

**What it measures**: Overall collective benefit across all dimensions

**Formula**:
```
Score = (
  Engagement × 0.2 +
  Wealth Distribution × 0.2 +
  Collective Action × 0.2 +
  Transparency × 0.15 +
  Participation × 0.15 +
  Accessibility × 0.1
)
```

**Target**: 75+  
**Frequency**: Daily  
**Dashboard**: Collective Benefit Dashboard

**How to improve**:
- Focus on lowest-scoring dimensions
- Set quarterly improvement goals
- Track progress over time
- Celebrate improvements
